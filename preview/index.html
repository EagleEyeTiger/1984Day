Why we're rallying

NSA surveillance is unconstitutional

Recent revelations about the NSA's surveillance infrastructure have revealed that phone and email records of US and foreign nationals are being collected and stored by the government.

The level of surveillance occuring presnetly is an abuse of the rights guaranteed by the Fourth Amendment and the United Nation's Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

Privacy matters and should not be compromised

Privacy is not simply a privelege, but a necessary precondition for a free and democratic society. In the words of Benjamin Franklin, “Those who would give up essential Liberty, to purchase a little temporary Safety, deserve neither Liberty nor Safety.”

We expect our government to protect our right to privacy with respect to digital communications data.

Restoring the Fourth Amendment

We ask that our representatives in Congress to restore the privacy guaranteed to US citizens by restoring the Fourth Amendment and passing legislation to revoke the NSA's ability to conduct mass surveillance of US and foreign citizens.
